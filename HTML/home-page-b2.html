Sri Lanka has 4000 species of flowering plants, 107 species of freshwater fish, 59 species of amphibians, 174 species of reptiles, 435 species of birds, 140 species of mammals, and thousands of invertebrates, according to its species diversity.

Sri Lanka is wooded on 29.9% of its land area, or around 1,933,000 hectares. Primary forest, the most biodiverse kind of forest, accounts for 8.6% of the total, or around 167,000 hectares. This equates to a 1.14 percent yearly deforestation rate.
